Core Architecture

One pipeline. Raw signal in, a safe route out.

Three automated layers translate raw satellite sensor files into a live, navigable mission map.

INPUT

Chandrayaan‑2 Data

DFSAR radar + OHRC optical imagery

LAYER 01

Volumetric Ice Detection Core

CPR > 1.0 & DOP < 0.13 → dielectric modelling → ice volume in m³

LAYER 02

Morphological Hazard Mapping

Slope > 15° and obstacles > 0.5 m masked as no-go zones

LAYER 03

Thermal-Aware Route Engine

A*/D* Lite "Shadow Hopping" with solar charging pitstops

OUTPUT

Mission Control Dashboard

Live ice, hazard & route layers + LMRS score

Core Features

Every number below is a real constraint from the explainer session — not a placeholder.

Volumetric Estimation Core

Estimates ice depth and volume down to 5 metres using dielectric modelling on DFSAR data — pure dust sits near ε≈2.5, ice mixtures shift to ε≈3.5.

CPR > 1.0DOP < 0.130–5 m profile

Terrain Hazard Masking

Uses DEMs and OHRC imagery to flag terrain that's physically dangerous: slopes beyond 15° and boulders or micro-craters larger than 0.5 m.

Slope > 15° = no-goObstacle > 0.5 m
Thermal Optimization

Shadow-Hopping Pathfinder

A*/D* Lite treats darkness as a heavy cost, not just distance — scheduling solar charging pitstops on sunlit rims before every dive into a 25 K crater.
